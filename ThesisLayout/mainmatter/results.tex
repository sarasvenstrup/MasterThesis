\chapter{Evaluation of the Arbitrage-Free Autoencoder}


This section evaluates the arbitrage-free autoencoder model described in Chapter \ref{chap:encoderdecodermodel}, fitted to swap rate curves across nine currencies (Chapter \ref{chap:datadescription}). The evaluation proceeds in two stages. First, in-sample performance is assessed across 2, 3, and 4 factor specifications. Second, out-of-sample reconstruction is evaluated using a rolling window scheme and benchmarked against the Extended Kalman Filter Dynamic Nelson-Siegel model.



% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{In-Sample Evaluation}
% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

In-sample evaluation serves as the first checkpoint toward establishing whether the model is practically useful. Before assessing out-of-sample reconstruction, the model must demonstrate that it can accurately reconstruct the swap rate curves it was trained on, and that the implied zero-coupon bond prices satisfy the no-arbitrage constraint. Following \citet{MatinWanPoulsen2025}, the reconstruction accuracy is measured by the RMSE in basis points, and the compliance with no-arbitrage is assessed through \textcolor{red}{the approximate Sharpe ratio/residual (REFERENCE)} of the implied zero-coupon bond prices, which should be negligible if the constraint is satisfied by construction.


\subsection{Reconstruction Accuracy}


%The hyperparameters should be shown in a table in the numerical implementation part.

We begin by examining how reconstruction error evolves over 5,000 training epochs. Figure~\ref{fig:training_loss} shows the average training RMSE across
 all currencies for $\ell \in \{1,2,3,4\}$. Both $\ell=3$ and $\ell=4$ exhibit notable spikes around 2 \,500 epochs, possibly reflecting a transition in the optimization trajectory, after which the training becomes more stable. By epoch 3\,500 all models have settled into a stable training regime with considerably reduced fluctuations, confirming that all models have fully converged. However, it should be noted that these loss curves reflect a single random initialization, and convergence behavior may vary between seeds, as explored further in Section~\textcolor{red}{INSERT REFERENCE}. With convergence established, we now assess the accuracy of the final reconstruction in all model specifications.

Having established that the model reconstructs swap rate curves reasonably well overall, while struggling at the short and long ends of the curve, during periods of structural market shifts, and particularly for EUR and JPY, we now turn to the second success criterion: whether the implied zero-coupon bond prices satisfy the no-arbitrage constraint.


\subsection{No-Arbitrage Diagnostic}


Reconstruction accuracy alone is insufficient if the implied bond prices permit arbitrage opportunities. Figure~\ref{fig:Q7_sharpe} therefore examines whether the model satisfies the no-arbitrage constraint, showing the bond pricing residual for $\ell=3$, averaged per currency across all in-sample dates and maturities. Two features of the figure confirm that the no-arbitrage constraint is satisfied. First, the scale of the y-axis is on the order of $10^{-9}$, indicating that the deviations from zero are neglible in magnitude. Second, the values fluctuate randomly around zero across all maturities and currencies, consistent with numerical noise rather than a systematic arbitrage violation. As this property holds by construction for all model specifications, the autoencoder can be considered arbitrage-free in practice.

\section{Out-of-Sample Evaluation}
% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Satisfactory in-sample reconstruction is a necessary but not sufficient condition for a useful model. In particular, a model with many parameters may overfit the training period, failing to preserve its reconstruction accuracy when applied to independent observations \textcolor{red}{(Reference to RegressionbyR Introduction - 1.2)}. To assess whether the autoencoder generalizes beyond the training period, out-of-sample reconstruction is evaluated using a rolling window scheme across 16 non-overlapping test periods, benchmarked against the Extended Kalman Filter Dynamic Nelson-Siegel model (EKF DNS). The evaluation uses 3,500 training epochs, consistent with the convergence analysis in Figure~\ref{fig:training_loss}.


\subsection{Rolling Window Evaluation}

Out-of-sample reconstruction is evaluated using a rolling window design, illustrated in Figure~\ref{fig:rolling_window_diagram}, yielding 16 non-overlapping test periods spanning January 2015 to December 2023. The same rolling window scheme is used to benchmark the autoencoder against the Extended Kalman Filter Dynamic Nelson Siegel model (EKF DNS). \textcolor{red}{(Find ud af hvornår vi skal introducere benchmarket, og argumentere for hvorfor det er netop denne vi har valgt)}

As shown in Figure~\ref{fig:Q2b}, while $\ell=2$ shows a modest increase from an in-sample RMSE of 12.67 bps to an out-of-sample RMSE of 22.3 bps, $\ell=3$ and $\ell=4$ produce catastrophically high average out-of-sample errors of 219.2 and 403.3 bps respectively, despite in-sample RMSEs of 10.33 and \textcolor{red}{X} bps.

The currency-level breakdown in Table~\ref{tab:Q4a} reveals where the explosions are most severe, with $\ell=3$ reaching 321.47 bps for GBP and $\ell=4$ reaching 471.4 bps for JPY. Figure~\ref{fig:Q3b} further reveals that the autoencoder explosions are concentrated around periods of market stress, most notably the ECB QE period in 2015 and the COVID shock in 2020, with more latent factors amplifying the instability.

Despite demonstrating reasonable in-sample reconstruction accuracy, the out-of-sample results paint a concerning picture, with the frequency and severity of the explosions increasing with the number of latent dimensions. The observed instability is hypothesized to stem from the absence of theoretical conditions imposed on the neural networks governing the drift and diffusion of the swap rate dynamics, which is explored further in the following chapter.
