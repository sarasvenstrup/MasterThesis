Table~\ref{tab:train_rmse} shows that the two-factor model outperforms the three-factor specification in terms of average RMSE. Overall error levels are modest, indicating that a low-dimensional latent representation captures most of the cross-sectional variation in swap curves.
Interestingly, introducing a third latent factor does not systematically improveme reconstruction accuracy. The marginal benefit of the extra dimension appears limited, suggesting that the first two latent factors already capture the primary level and slope movements of the yield curve. 


\textcolor{red}{Conclusion: We have learned that when we extend the model, it is important to let the model run for more epochs relative to when it was only two dimensions to see good results. This is due to the more weights and parameters that the model must take into account.}

Importantly, the decoder enforces no-arbitrage as a hard constraint through the ODE system for zero coupon bond prices. While this reduces flexibility relative to unconstrained autoencoders, the empirical results indicate that the associated loss in cross-sectional fit is modest. The model therefore achieves competitive reconstruction accuracy while ensuring internally consistent bond price dynamics by construction.
