\chapter{Affine-Inspired Arbitrage-Free Encoder--Decoder Framework}


In this chapter, we present the affine-inspired encoder--decoder model proposed by \citet{MatinWanPoulsen2025}. The model is structured as a neural network architecture consisting of an encoder and decoder. Its objective is to reconstruct par swap rate curves while enforcing the risk-neutral no-arbitrage condition by construction.\\
\\
The encoder maps observed swap rates to a low-dimensional latent state representation. The decoder transforms this latent state into a continuous zero-coupon bond price curve consistent with arbitrage-free term structure dynamics. Since par swap rates are deterministic functions of zero-coupon bond prices, the reconstructed swap rates inherit the arbitrage-freeness.
Figure~\ref{fig:rolf_architecture} provides a graphical overview of the architecture.

We next present a more detailed description of the framework's individual components, beginning with the linear encoder.

\section{Linear Encoding}

We consider observed par swap rates at time $t$ on the maturity grid
\[
M=\{1\text{Y},2\text{Y},3\text{Y},5\text{Y},10\text{Y},15\text{Y},20\text{Y},30\text{Y}\}.
\]
which are assembled into the input vector
\[
\mathbf{S}_t=(S_1(t),\dots,S_8(t))^\top \in \mathbb{R}^8.
\]
Following \citet{MatinWanPoulsen2025}, who consider a latent factor representation of dimension $l=2$, we formulate the model using a general latent dimension $l$ to allow for later extension. The encoder then performs a linear transformation of the observed swap rates,
\[
z_t = A_1 \mathbf{S}_t,
\]
where $A_1 \in \mathbb{R}^{l \times 8}$ and $z_t=(z_1(t),...,z_l(t))^\top \in \mathbb{R}^l$.









\section{Swap Rate Reconstruction from Bond Prices}

Before turning to the internal structure of the decoder, we note that its task is to generate zero-coupon bond prices $P(z_t,\tau)$ on the annually spaced maturity grid
\[
\tau \in \{1\text{Y},2\text{Y},\dots,30\text{Y}\}.
\]
Ignoring basis spreads between discounting and forwarding curves, the reconstructed par swap rate with maturity $\tau$ is given by
\begin{equation}
\tilde{S}(z_t,\tau)
=
\frac{1-P(z_t,\tau)}
{\sum_{\tau' \leq \tau} P(z_t,\tau')}.
\label{estimatedswaprates}
\end{equation}
where the sum runs over all maturities on the specified grid less than or equal to $\tau$.


Since swap rates are linear functionals of ZCB prices, arbitrage consistency must be enforced at the level of bond prices. The decoder is therefore constructed to generate bond prices from the latent factors $z_t$ such that the risk-neutral no-arbitrage condition is satisfied. As a consequence, the reconstructed swap rates obtained from \ref{estimatedswaprates} are arbitrage-free by construction.


\section{Affine Bond Prices}

Having specified the input and output of the model, we now \textcolor{red}{examine} the internal structure of the decoder in more detail. While \ref{fig:rolf_architecture} illustrates the overall mapping from latent factors to bond prices and swap rates, several additional components operate beneath this visualization.

Strictly speaking, the decoder does not generate ZCB prices directly. Instead, as illustrated \ref{fig:rolf_architecture_details}, it first produces a scalar function $\mathcal{G}(z_t,\tau)$ which depends on the latent state $z_t$ and maturity $\tau$. The bond prices are then obtained indirectly through an affine-inspired representation.

\subsection{Affine-inspired bond price representation}

Following \cite{MatinWanPoulsen2025}, ZCB-prices are assumed to admit an affine-like structure. For fixed time $t$, the time-$t$ ZCB price with time-to-maturity $\tau$ is written as

\begin{equation}
    P(z_t,\tau)
=
\exp\!\Big(A_{z_t}(\tau)-B_{z_t}(\tau)\,G(z_t,\tau)\Big),
\label{affineinspiredzcbprices}
\end{equation}

where $z_t$ is the latent state for fixed $t$, $A_{z_t}(\tau)$ and $B_{z_t}(\tau)$ are scalar functions of $\tau$,
and $\mathcal{G}(z_t,\tau)$ is a scalar function that is learned.

For every encoded latent state $z_t$, the functions $A_{z_t}(\tau)$ and $B_{z_t}(\tau)$ are generated anew by solving the associated no-arbitrage ODE system. They are therefore state-dependent objects rather than universal functions shared across time.


The terminology \textit{affine-inspired} reflects the similarity to classical affine term structure models, where bond prices are exponential-affine in latent factors.

\subsection{Neural Parametrization of the dynamics}

To evaluate the bond prices, the functions $A_{z_t}$ and $B_{z_t}$ must be computed. These are obtained by solving a system of coupled ODEs derived from the no-arbitrage condition. The coefficients in the ODE depend on drift and volatility of the latent factors, the short rate and the derivatives of $\mathcal{G(z_t,\tau))$. 

All model components required for this evaluation are parametrized by neural networks and learned jointly with $\mathcal{G}$.

Formally, the networks are defined as follows:
The drift network
\begin{equation}
    \mathcal{K}: 
    \begin{pmatrix}
    z_1(t)\\
    \vdots\\
    z_l(t)    
    \end{pmatrix}\longrightarrow 
    \begin{pmatrix}
    \mu_1(t)\\
    \vdots\\
    \mu_l(t)    
    \end{pmatrix}=\textbf{M}
    \begin{pmatrix}
    z_1(t)\\
    \vdots\\
    z_l(t)    
    \end{pmatrix}+\textbf{N}
\end{equation}

The volatility network
\begin{equation}
    \mathcal{H}: 
    \begin{pmatrix}
    z_1(t)\\
    \vdots\\
    z_l(t)    
    \end{pmatrix}\longrightarrow 
    \begin{pmatrix}
    \log(\sigma_{11})\\
    \vdots\\
    \log(\sigma_{ll})\\
    \text{atanh}(\rho_{11})\\
    \vdots\\
    \text{atanh}(\rho_{ll})
    \end{pmatrix}
\end{equation}

The short rate network
\begin{equation}
    \mathcal{R}: 
    \begin{pmatrix}
    z_1(t)\\
    \vdots\\
    z_l(t)    
    \end{pmatrix}\longrightarrow 
    \tilde{r}
\end{equation}

In all networks, the activation function in the latent layer is the centered soft step function 
$$\psi(x)=\frac{1}{1+e^{-x}}-\frac{1}{2}$$

\subsection{Overall decoder mechanism}

The decoder therefore operates as follows. The latent factors $z_t$ are first mapped to the function $\mathcal{G}(z_t,\tau)$. Together with the neural-network-parametrized drift, volatility and short rate functions, this determines a system of no-arbitrage ODEs. Solving this ODE system yields the functions $A_{z_t}(\tau)$ and $B_{z_t}(\tau)$, from which the ZCB-prices are obtained. The prices are finally converted into model-implied swap rates using \eqref{estimatedswaprates}

In this way, the decoder transforms the latent factors into arbitrage-free ZCB prices and, consequently, arbitrage-free swap rates.

\section{Motivation for Arbitrage-Free Decoding}

To ensure that the generated bond price curve satisfies the no-arbitrage condition by construction, we parameterize bond prices in an affine-inspired form and derive a system of ordinary differential equations (ODEs) for each encoded latent state $z_t$. The derivation of this arbitrage-free structure is presented below.

We begin by specifying the short rate. Recall from \eqref{shortrate} that the short rate is defined as the instantaneous forward rate at zero maturity. Under the affine-inspired bond price representation \eqref{affineinspiredzcbprices}, the instantaneous forward rate is given by
\begin{align*}
    f(t,\tau)&=-\frac{\partial\log(P(z_t,\tau))}{\partial \tau}\\
    &=-\partial_{\tau}A_{z_t}(\tau)+\partial_{\tau}B_{z_t}(\tau)\mathcal{G}(z_t,\tau)+\partial_{\tau}\mathcal{G}(z_t,\tau)B_{z_t}(\tau)
\end{align*}
Taking the limit as $\tau\rightarrow0$ therefore implies that the short rate must be a function of the latent state $z_t$.

Furthermore, comparing the affine-inspired representation \eqref{affineinspiredzcbprices} with the risk neutral pricing formula \eqref{riskneturalbondpriceformula} shows that the bond prices depend only on the current latent state. Hence, under absence of arbitrage, the \textcolor{red}{latent factors(or latent state???)} $z_t$ must form a Markov process under the risk-neutral measure $\mathbb{Q}$.

It is therefore natural to impose diffusion dynamics on the latent factors and to model the short rate as a continuous transformation of $z_t$:
\begin{equation}
    dz_t=\mu(z_t)dt+\sigma(z_t)dW_t^\mathbb{Q}
    \label{latentdynamics}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
    r(t)=\tilde{r}(z_t)
    \label{shortratedynamic}
\end{equation}
where $W_t^\mathbb{Q}$ is a standard Brownian motion under the risk neutral measure $\mathbb{Q}$.
