\chapter[Affine-Inspired Arbitrage-Free Encoder-Decoder Framework]
{Affine-Inspired Arbitrage-Free\\Encoder-Decoder Framework}
\label{chap:encoderdecodermodel}

This chapter presents the affine-inspired encoder--decoder model proposed by \citet{MatinWanPoulsen2025}, formulated here in a generalized framework that accommodates an arbitrary number of latent factors and currencies. The model combines a low-dimensional latent representation with an arbitrage-free bond-pricing structure in order to reconstruct observed par swap rate curves while enforcing the risk-neutral no-arbitrage condition by construction. \textcolor{red}{MANGLER}

The encoder maps observed swap rates into a low-dimensional latent state representation. The decoder then transforms this latent state into a continuous zero-coupon bond price curve consistent with arbitrage-free term structure dynamics. Since par swap rates are deterministic functions of zero-coupon bond prices, the reconstructed swap rates are arbitrage-free whenever the underlying bond prices satisfy the no-arbitrage condition. Figure~\ref{fig:rolf_architecture} provides a graphical overview of the architecture.

The remainder of the chapter describes the individual components of the framework in detail, beginning with the linear encoder.

\section{Linear Encoder}

We consider observed par swap rates at time $t$ on the discrete maturity grid
\[
\{\tau_1,\tau_2,\dots,\tau_M\}, \qquad 0<\tau_1<\tau_2<\dots<\tau_M,
\]
where $S_t^{(i)}:=S(t,\tau_i)$ denotes the par swap rate for tenor $\tau_i$. These are collected in the input vector
\[
\mathbf{S}_t=(S_1(t),\dots,S_M(t))^\top \in \mathbb{R}^M.
\]
The encoder maps the observed swap curve into a low-dimensional latent state through a linear transformation,
\[
z_t = A_1 \mathbf{S}_t,
\]
where $A_1 \in \mathbb{R}^{\ell \times M}$ and
\[
z_t=(z_1(t),\dots,z_\ell(t))^\top \in \mathbb{R}^\ell.
\]
Thus, the encoder compresses the observed swap curve into an $\ell$-dimensional latent representation that serves as the input to the decoder.




\section{Decoder}

With the latent state $z_t$ in hand, the decoder is constructed to map it directly to zero-coupon bond prices $P(z_t,\tau)$. As shown in \textcolor{red}{(Section X)}, the absence of arbitrage implies that bond prices depend on calendar time only through the latent state, so that $P(t,T)=P(z_t,\tau)$ with $\tau=T-t$. 

\subsection{Swap Rate Reconstruction from Bond Prices}

Before describing the internal structure of the decoder, we note that its task is to generate zero-coupon bond prices $P(z_t,\tau)$ on the annually spaced maturity grid
\[
\tau \in \{1\text{Y},2\text{Y},\dots,30\text{Y}\}.
\]
Following \cite{MatinWanPoulsen2025}, we adopt a single-curve framework and assume annual fixed-leg payments, setting accrual factors $\Delta_{\tau'}=1$ for all grid points. The reconstructed par swap rate with maturity $\tau$ is then given by
\begin{equation}
\tilde{S}(z_t,\tau)
=
\frac{1-P(z_t,\tau)}
{\sum_{\tau' \leq \tau} P(z_t,\tau')}.
\label{estimatedswaprates}
\end{equation}
where the sum runs over all maturities on the specified grid less than or equal to $\tau$. Since swap rates are nonlinear functionals of zero-coupon bond prices, the decoder is constructed to generate bond prices satisfying the risk-neutral no-arbitrage condition, so that the reconstructed swap rates inherit arbitrage-freeness by construction.

\subsection{The Decoder Network}
%--------------------------------------------------------
Having specified the input and output of the model, we now describe the internal structure of the decoder in detail. Figure~\ref{fig:rolf_architecture} illustrates the overall mapping from latent factors to bond prices, however the decoder consists of additional components not explicitly shown in this high-level representation. As illustrated in the more detailed Figure~\ref{fig:rolf_architecture_details}, latent factors branch into four neural networks. Three of these, $\mathcal{K},\mathcal{H}$ and $\mathcal{R}$, are used for parameter modeling and are later discussed in section \textcolor{red}{(REFERENCE)}. Here we focus on $\mathcal{G}$, which together with the affine-inspired representation described in the following section produces ZCB prices.

The network $\mathcal{G}$ takes as input the augmented vector $(z_t^\top,\tau)^\top\in\mathbb{R}^{\ell+1}$ and consists of one hidden layer of dimension 10, with hidden units $h_1,...,h_{10}$ as illustrated in Figure \ref{fig:rolf_architecture_details}, where the hidden layer dimension was determined empirically in \cite{MatinWanPoulsen2025}. The network computed
$$\mathcal{G}(z_t,\tau)=\psi\bigg(W_2\underbrace{\psi\left(W_1
(z_t^\top,\tau)^\top+b_1\right)}_{=h}+b_2\bigg)$$
where $W_1\in\mathbb{R}^{10\times(\ell+1)},b_1\in\mathbb{R}^{10},W_2\in\mathbb{R}^{1\times 10}$ and $b_2\in\mathbb{R}$ are learnable parameters, and $\psi$ denotes the centered soft-step activation function
\begin{equation}
    \psi(x)=\frac{1}{1+e^{-x}}-\frac{1}{2}.
    \label{eq:activation}
\end{equation}



%--------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Affine-Inspired Bond Price Representation}
%--------------------------------------------------------

Following \citet{MatinWanPoulsen2025}, zero-coupon bond prices are assumed to admit an affine-inspired structure. The price of a zero-coupon bond with time-to-maturity $\tau$ is given by
\begin{equation}
P(z_t,\tau)
=
\exp\!\Big(A_{z_t}(\tau)-B_{z_t}(\tau)\,\mathcal{G}(z_t,\tau)\Big),
\label{affineinspiredzcbprices}
\end{equation}
where $A_{z_t}(\tau),B_{z_t}(\tau)\in\mathbb{R}$ are scalar functions of maturity $\tau$ that depend on $z_t$ through the model parameters. The term \textit{affine-inspired} reflects the structural analogy to classical affine term structure models, in which bond prices are exponential-affine in the state vector (see Section \ref{sec:Affine TSM Theory}). Here, the learned factor $\mathcal{G}(z_t,\tau)$ captures nonlinear dynamics while retaining the  exponential-affine form.


For each latent state $z_t$, the functions $A_{z_t}(\tau)$ and $B_{z_t}(\tau)$ are obtained by solving a coupled ODE system derived from the no-arbitrage condition. These are state-specific solutions, meaning that a new ODE system must be solved for every newly encoded latent state, thereby enforcing no-arbitrage consistency for each newly encoded latent state.

The derivation of this ODE system, which ensures that the resulting zero-coupon bond prices satisfy the risk-neutral no-arbitrage condition, is the subject of the following section.


%--------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Derivation of the No-Arbitrage ODE System}
%--------------------------------------------------------

%To guarantee that the generated bond price curve satisfies the no-arbitrage condition by construction, we parameterize bond prices in an affine-inspired form and derive a system of ordinary differential equations (ODEs) that must be satisfied for each realized latent state $z_t$. These ODEs arise directly from the risk-neutral pricing condition and enforce arbitrage-freeness at the level of zero-coupon bond prices.

We begin by specifying the short rate. Recall from \eqref{shortrate} that the short rate is defined as the instantaneous forward rate at zero maturity. Under the affine-inspired bond price representation \eqref{affineinspiredzcbprices}, the instantaneous forward rate is given by
\begin{equation}
    f(t,\tau)=-\frac{\partial\log(P(z_t,\tau))}{\partial \tau}\\
    =-\partial_{\tau}A_{z_t}(\tau)+\partial_{\tau}B_{z_t}(\tau)\mathcal{G}(z_t,\tau)+\partial_{\tau}\mathcal{G}(z_t,\tau)B_{z_t}(\tau)
\end{equation}
Taking the limit as $\tau \to 0$ and recalling that 
$r(t)=\lim_{\tau\to0}f(t,\tau)$
shows that the short rate is determined entirely by the current latent state $z_t$. We therefore define the short-rate function $\tilde r:\mathbb{R}^\ell\to\mathbb{R}$ such that
\[
r(t)=\tilde r(z_t).
\]

% Furthermore, comparing the affine-inspired representation \eqref{affineinspiredzcbprices} with the risk-neutral pricing formula \eqref{riskneturalbondpriceformula} shows that the bond prices depend only on the current latent state. Hence, under absence of arbitrage, the latent state vector $z_t$ must form a Markov process under the risk-neutral measure $\mathbb{Q}$.

Moreover, the affine-inspired representation \eqref{affineinspiredzcbprices} implies that bond prices depend only on the current latent state $z_t$. 
Since under the risk-neutral measure bond prices admit the representation
\[
P(t,t+\tau)=\mathbb{E}^{\mathbb{Q}}\!\left[\exp\!\left(-\int_t^{t+\tau} \tilde{r}(z_u)\,du\right)\,\bigg|\,\mathcal{F}_t\right],
\]
consistency requires that the conditional expectation depends on $\mathcal{F}_t$ only through $z_t$, which is precisely the Markov property. Absence of arbitrage therefore implies that $z_t$ must follow a Markov process under $\mathbb{Q}$. We may therefore write $P(t,T)=P(z_t,\tau)$ with $\tau=T-t$.



% It is therefore natural to impose diffusion dynamics on the latent factors and to model the short rate as a continuous transformation of $z_t$:

We therefore model the latent state as a Markov process under the risk-neutral measure. Following \cite{MatinWanPoulsen2025}, we further specify the dynamics as a diffusion:
\begin{equation}
    dz_t=\mu(z_t)dt+\sigma(z_t)dW_t^\mathbb{Q}
    \label{latentdynamics}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
    r(t)=\tilde{r}(z_t)
    \label{shortratedynamic}
\end{equation}
where $\mu(z_t)\in\mathbb{R}^\ell$ is the drift vector, $\sigma(z_t)\in\mathbb{R}^{\ell\times \ell}$ is the volatility matrix, and $W_t^\mathbb{Q}$ is an $\ell$-dimensional standard Brownian motion under the risk-neutral measure $\mathbb{Q}$.


 
The short rate admits two representations that must coincide identically: it is defined as the instantaneous forward rate at zero maturity
\[
r(t)=\lim_{\tau\to0} f(t,\tau),
\]
while at the same time it is modeled as $r(t)=\tilde{r}(z_t)$. Unlike approaches that enforce no-arbitrage softly through a penalty term in the loss function \textcolor{red}{(REFERENCE)}, this coincidence constitutes a \textit{hard constraint}. When deriving the ODE system for $A_{z_t}$ and $B_{z_t}$ from the bond-pricing PDE, the coefficients are constructed such that
\[
\lim_{\tau\to0} f(t,\tau)=\tilde r(z_t)
\]
holds exactly for every state $z_t$, thereby embedding the short rate specification directly into the arbitrage-free structure.

With the dynamics and short-rate specification in place, we can now derive the bond-pricing PDE. 


Assuming sufficient smoothness of $P$, in particular that $P$ is twice continuously differentiable in $z$ and once in $\tau$, we apply Itô's lemma. Since $d\tau=-dt$
\begin{align}
dP(z_t,\tau)
&=
\left(
-\partial_\tau P(z_t,\tau)
+
\nabla_{z_t} P(z_t,\tau)^\top \mu(z_t)
+
\frac12 \mathrm{Tr}\!\big[\sigma(z_t)\sigma(z_t)^\top H_{z_t} P(z_t,\tau)\big]
\right) dt\nonumber\\
&\phantom{=}+
\nabla_z P(z_t,\tau)^\top \sigma(z_t)\, dW_t^{\mathbb Q}. 
\label{Pdynamics}
\end{align}
Under absence of arbitrage, the discounted bond price process 
\(
\tilde{P}(z_t,\tau) := \frac{P(z_t,\tau)}{B(t)}
\)
must be a martingale under $\mathbb Q$, which requires its drift to vanish. Applying the product rule for semimartingales, 
\begin{align*}
   d\tilde{P}(z_t,\tau)&=\frac{1}{B(t)}dP(z_t,\tau)+(z_t,\tau)d\left(\frac{1}{B(t)}\right)+d\left\langle P(z_t,\tau),\frac{1}{B(t)}\right\rangle \\
    &=\frac{1}{B(t)}dP(z_t,\tau)-r(t)\frac{P(z_t,\tau)}{B(t)}dt
\end{align*}
and substituting \eqref{Pdynamics} gives, suppressing the arguments of $P$ and $\tilde{P}$ for readability,
\begin{align*}
  d\tilde{P}&=\frac{1}{B(t)}\left(-\partial_\tau P+\nabla_z P^\top\mu(z_t)+\frac{1}{2}Tr\left[\sigma^\top(z_t)H_zP\sigma(z_t)\right]-r(t)P\right)dt\\
  &\textcolor{white}{=}+\frac{1}{B(t)}\nabla_zP^\top\sigma(z_t)dW_t^\mathbb{Q}  
\end{align*}
Setting the drift to zero and using $r(t)=\tilde{r}(z_t)$ from \eqref{shortratedynamic} yields the bond-pricing PDE 
\[
-\partial_\tau P
+
\nabla_z P^\top \mu(z_t)
+
\frac12 \mathrm{Tr}\!\left[\sigma(z_t)\sigma(z_t)^\top H_z P\right]
-
\tilde r(z_t)\,P
=
0
\]

with boundary condition $P(z_t,0)=1$. 

Substituting the derivatives under the ansatz \eqref{affineinspiredzcbprices}, derived in Appendix~\ref{sec:PDE Derivations Helper},
\begin{align}
    \partial_\tau P
&=
\Big(
\partial_\tau A_{z_t}
-
(\partial_\tau B_{z_t})\,\mathcal{G}
-
B_{z_t}\,\partial_\tau \mathcal{G}
\Big)P\\
\nabla_{z_t} P
&=
(\nabla_{z_t} \Phi)\,P=
\big(-B_{z_t}\,\nabla_{z_t} \mathcal{G}\big)P\\
H_{z_t} P&=
P\Big(
-B_{z_t}\,H_z \mathcal{G}
+
B_{z_t}^2\,\nabla_{z_t} \mathcal{G}\,\nabla_{z_t} \mathcal{G}^\top
\Big)
\end{align}
into the PDE and factoring out $P>0$, and rearranging
\[
-\partial_\tau A_{z_t} + (\partial_\tau B_{z_t})\,\mathcal{G} + B_{z_t}\,\partial_\tau\mathcal{G} - B_{z_t}(\nabla_{z_t}\mathcal{G})^\top\mu(z_t) - \tfrac{1}{2}B_{z_t}\,\mathrm{Tr}\!\left[\sigma\sigma^\top H_{z_t}\mathcal{G}\right] + \tfrac{1}{2}B_{z_t}^2\,\mathrm{Tr}\!\left[\sigma\sigma^\top\nabla_{z_t}\mathcal{G}\,\nabla_{z_t}\mathcal{G}^\top\right] - \tilde{r}(z_t) = 0.
\]
We then define the scalar coefficient functions
\begin{align}
\alpha_{z_t}(\tau)&:=\frac{-(\partial_\tau \mathcal{G})+(\nabla_{z_t} \mathcal{G})^\top \mu({z_t})+\tfrac12 \mathrm{Tr}\!\big[\sigma({z_t})\sigma({z_t})^\top H_{z_t} \mathcal{G}\big]
}{\mathcal{G}}\\
\beta_{z_t}(\tau)&:=\frac{\tilde r(z_t)}{\mathcal{G}}\\
\gamma_{z_t}(\tau)&:=
\frac12\,\mathrm{Tr}\!\Big[\sigma({z_t})\sigma({z_t})^\top \nabla_{z_t} \mathcal{G}\,\nabla_{z_t} \mathcal{G}^\top\Big]
\end{align}
whereby the identity reduces to
\[
\partial_\tau A_{z_t} = \mathcal{G}\big(\partial_\tau B_{z_t} - \beta_{z_t}(\tau) - \alpha_{z_t}(\tau) B_{z_t}\big) + B_{z_t}^2\,\gamma_{z_t}(\tau).
\]
For this to hold for all $\tau\geq0$, a sufficient choice is
\begin{align}
\partial_\tau B_{z_t}(\tau) &= \alpha_{z_t}(\tau)\,B_{z_t}(\tau) + \beta_{z_t}(\tau),\qquad B(0)=0\\
\partial_\tau A_{z_t}(\tau) &= B_{z_t}(\tau)^2\,\gamma_{z_t}(\tau),\qquad \qquad\;\;\;\;\; A(0)=0
\end{align}
where the boundary condition follows from $P(z_t,0)=\exp(A_{z_t}(0)-B_{z_t}(0)\mathcal{G}(z_t,0))=1$.



This system of coupled ODEs can be reduced to a single ODE by introducing a vector $X_{z_t}(\tau)=(A_{z_t}(\tau),B_{z_t}(\tau))^\top$ where $X_{z_t}(0)=(0,0)^\top$,
\begin{align*}
    \partial_{\tau}X_{z_t}(\tau)&=
    \begin{pmatrix}
     \partial_{\tau}A_{z_t}(\tau)\\
     \partial_{\tau}B_{z_t}(\tau)   
    \end{pmatrix}=\begin{pmatrix}
     B_{z_t}(\tau)^2\gamma_{z_t}(\tau)\\
     B_{z_t}(\tau)\alpha_{z_t}(\tau)+\beta_{z_t}(\tau)  
    \end{pmatrix}\\
    &=\begin{pmatrix}
        0 & \gamma_{z_t}(\tau)\\
        0 & 0
    \end{pmatrix}
    \underbrace{\begin{pmatrix}
     A_{z_t}(\tau)^2\\
     B_{z_t}(\tau)^2   
    \end{pmatrix}}_{X_{z_t}(\tau)\odot X_{z_t}(\tau) }+
    \begin{pmatrix}
        0 & 0\\
        0 & \alpha_{z_t}(\tau)
    \end{pmatrix}
    \begin{pmatrix}
     A_{z_t}(\tau)\\
     B_{z_t}(\tau)   
    \end{pmatrix}+
    \begin{pmatrix}
     0\\
     \beta_{z_t}(\tau)   
    \end{pmatrix}
\end{align*}
where $\odot$ denotes elementwise multiplication. This system is solved numerically for each realized latent state $z_t$ using a fourth-order Runge--Kutta scheme (3/8-rule), yielding $A_{z_t}(\tau)$ and $B_{z_t}(\tau)$ such that the resulting bond prices \eqref{affineinspiredzcbprices} satisfy the no-arbitrage condition for all maturities $\tau$.

\subsection{Neural Modelling of the Dynamics}

The coefficients $\alpha_{z_t}(\tau),\beta_{z_t}(\tau)$, and $\gamma_{z_t}(\tau)$ of the no-arbitrage ODE system depend on the drift $\mu(z_t)$, the volatility $\sigma(z_t)$, and the short rate $\tilde{r}(z_t)$. Following \citet{MatinWanPoulsen2025}, and as illustrated in Figure \ref{fig:rolf_architecture_details}, each of these is modeled by a dedicated neural network learned jointly with $\mathcal{G}$. The drift network $\mathcal{K}$ includes a bias term, while $\mathcal{H}$ and $\mathcal{R}$ have no bias terms, and all three networks use the centered soft-step activation function $\psi$ from \eqref{eq:activation}.


\paragraph{Drift network}

The drift of the latent factors is modeled by a a neural network $\mathcal{K}:\mathbb{R}^\ell\rightarrow\mathbb{R}^\ell$, which performs the affine transformation
\[
\mathcal{K} :
\begin{pmatrix}
z_1(t) \\
\vdots \\
z_\ell(t)
\end{pmatrix}
\;\mapsto\;
\begin{pmatrix}
\mu_1(z_t) \\
\vdots \\
\mu_\ell(z_t)
\end{pmatrix}
=
\mathbf{M}
\begin{pmatrix}
z_1(t) \\
\vdots \\
z_\ell(t)
\end{pmatrix}
+
\mathbf{N}
\]
where $\mathbf{M}\in\mathbb{R}^{\ell\times \ell}$ and $\mathbf{N}\in\mathbb{R}^\ell$. This affine structure allows the drift to capture mean-reverting dynamics in the latent factors.


\paragraph{Volatility network}
The volatility matrix is modeled by a neural network 
$\mathcal{H}:\mathbb{R}^{\ell}\rightarrow\mathbb{R}^{\ell+\ell(\ell-1)/2}$, with one hidden layer of dimension 4, which performs the mapping
\[
\mathcal{H} :
\begin{pmatrix}
z_1(t) \\
\vdots \\
z_\ell(t)
\end{pmatrix}
\;\mapsto\;
\begin{pmatrix}
\log(\sigma_1) \\
\vdots \\
\log(\sigma_\ell) \\
\operatorname{atanh}(\rho_{12}) \\
\vdots \\
\operatorname{atanh}(\rho_{(\ell-1)\ell})
\end{pmatrix}.
\]

The short rate is modeled as a neural network $\mathcal{R}:\mathbb{R}^\ell \to \mathbb{R}$ with dimensions $(\ell,4,1)$ that performs the mapping

\subsection{Full Decoding Pipeline}


To summarize, the decoder operates by firsy mapping the latent state $z_t$, through the four neural networks $\mathcal{G},\mathcal{K},\mathcal{H}$ and $\mathcal{R}$. The outpus determine the coefficients $\alpha_{z_t}(\tau),\beta_{z_t}(\tau)$ and $\gamma_{z_t}(\tau)$ of the no-arbitrage ODE system, which is solved numerically for each realized $z_t$ to yield $A_{z_t}(\tau)$ and $B_{z_t}(\tau)$. Zero-coupon bond prices are then obtained via \eqref{affineinspiredzcbprices} and converted into model-implied swap rates using \eqref{estimatedswaprates}, with arbitrage-freeness holding by construction throughout.
